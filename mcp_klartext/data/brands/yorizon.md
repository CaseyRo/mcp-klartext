# Yorizon

## Voice
- **Perspective:** "we" (the Yorizon team). NEVER "I" (the CDiT founder).
- **Register:** Professional Product Owner. Enterprise SaaS appropriate.
- **Tone:** Collaborative, team-oriented. Product decisions, not consulting pitches.

## Language
- **Primary:** English only.

## Visual
- **Aesthetic:** Enterprise SaaS. Professional, polished, not personal.
- **Palette:** Yorizon brand colors ONLY. No CDiT branding.
- **Typography:** System professional. Corporate-clean.
- **Photography:** Team collaboration, product screenshots, enterprise context.
- **Never:** Personal brand aesthetic, CDiT colors/logo, consulting language

## Hard Rules — Vertical Separation
- Casey is part of the team, not selling to it.
- Content stays in the Yorizon lane. No cross-pollination in public output.
- Horizontal learning from CDiT/casey.berlin experience is fine.
- Publishing is strictly vertical.
- NEVER pitch CDiT services, reference consulting work, or blur PO role with consultancy.
- NEVER use casey.berlin or CDiT visual language.

## Bleed Scan — Forbidden Vocabulary
Before finalizing ANY Yorizon content, scan for these terms and BLOCK if found:
- `consulting`, `consultancy`, `CDiT`, `cdit-works`, `CDIT`
- `casey.berlin`, `manifesto`, `Digitale Sprechstunde`
- `Nah?`, `StoryKeep` (other brand names)
- First-person `I` outside of team context (e.g., "I think" is ok in product discussion, "I offer consulting" is not)
- Any CDiT project identifiers (CDI-xxx)

If forbidden vocabulary detected → block output and explain: "Yorizon bleed detected: '[term]' found. Yorizon content must not reference CDiT services. Revise?"
